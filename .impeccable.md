## Design Context

### Users
People using a TV browser as a large ambient clock in a Korean living room. The job is to read the current KST time from across the room, occasionally check weather, and rely on calm timer/alarm reminders without turning the screen into a busy dashboard.

### Brand Personality
Quiet, composed, artful. The interface should feel like a restrained household object with a gallery-like backdrop, not a productivity app or decorative landing page.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark, cinematic, and legible from a distance. The default mode may mix time, weather, music, and art, while time focus mode should remove nonessential weather information and make the clock the primary visual object.

### Design Principles
- Prioritize long-distance readability over dense controls.
- Keep controls available but visually quiet until the user needs them.
- Use local assets and clear attribution so the TV experience does not depend on hotlinked media.
- Preserve calm motion and avoid attention-heavy effects except when an alarm needs to be noticed.
- Treat focus mode as a visual state, not a data reset: hidden weather information should remain intact.
